The earlier, the better...

While all children grow and develop at their own rate, some infants and toddlers experience delays in their development or have been diagnosed with a special health condition that is likely to lead to non-typical development.  The earlier any potential delays and disabilities are identified and early intervention services begin, the better chance a young child has of achieving his or her best potential. 

The overall goals of early intervention are to: 
Enable young children to be active and successful participants during their early childhood years and in the future in a variety of settings -- in their homes, in child care or preschool programs, and in their communities; and
Enable families to provide care for their children and have the resources they need to participate in their own desired family and community activities.

Frequenty Asked Questions

How do I know if my child's development is delayed?

Children develop in certain predictable ways, referred to as developmental milestones. There are specific behaviors and skills that can be expected to be seen in infants and toddlers--like rolling over, walking, or talking -- that are expected during a certain age range. These milestones help us understand if a child's development is on track or if she may need additional support.  Visit this page to view the developmental milestones expected for your child's age.

What is developmental screening?

Screening is a brief, simple process that helps answer the questions "Is this child's development typical for his age level?" and "Does this child need to be referred for a more in-depth evaluation?"

Do I need a doctor's referral to participate in the Maryland Infants and Toddlers Program?

While it is important to speak with your doctor about your concerns and questions about your child's development, a referral from your doctor is not necessary. Begin by filling out the online referral or calling the Infants and Toddlers Program in your county.

I am not comforatable sharing personal details online.  Is there a number I can call?

Absolutely! Every county in Maryland provides this service and staff is ready to assist you. Here is a list of phone numbers to reach the local Infants and Toddlers Program in the county in which you live.

Is there a cost to receive services from the Maryland Infants and Toddlers Program?

No, there is no cost to receive services from the Maryland Infants and Toddlers Program. Families who live in other states or in Washington, D.C., must contact their local programs for more information as some states do charge a fee.

Ready to Make a Referral?

Services are available to any eligible infant or toddler in every Maryland jurisdiction.  
Referrals to the Maryland Infants and Toddlers Program can be made for children ages birth to 36 months who reside in Maryland.
